Prayer Based Therapy

My current research seeks to reclaim and rescue the art of prayer and the act of praying from denominational segregation, and theological separatism, restoring its original condition as the first and foremost creative medium, through which every man, woman, and child may express their most authentic voice, whether in celebration or dire need, and experience the blessing of true solace in reply.

For health professionals and those called to a healing vocation, I teach a coherent and practicable form of Prayer Based Therapy that can complement the existing principles and practices used by practitioners of all orientations.

For those in search of healing, I seek to illuminate the benefits of prayer without recourse to religious dogma or theological doctrine.

The Creative Art of Prayer

Prayer was originally inseparable from creative expression, for the earliest forms of dance, music, and drama served the purpose of communicating with a Spiritual Presence.

Therefore, creativity and artistic expression are among the means by which we may lead people back to prayer in its original multiple forms, uniting previously disparate, disenfranchised, and conflicted groups, whilst relieving individuals of unnecessary pain.

Specifically, I have witnessed many people experience deep healing through the process of writing, choreographing, and giving voice to their own 'Primal Prayer'.

My work helps everyone excavate and express such a personal and primordial voice. For, that process is at the heart of Prayer Based Therapy.

In Times of Need

A lot of people pray when struck with significant mental or emotional difficulties. And many tend to reach for the help of a Spiritual Presence when troubles strike, even if only through a passing phrase, directed at the sky, such as 'Please Help Me'.

Sadly, the primordial healing power of true prayer, that could relieve widespread suffering, is for many people obscured by the intimidating doors of formal organized religion.

So we rely upon human interventions. And while psychiatrists, psychologists, therapists, and medication can help, they are sometimes unnecessary, often essential, yet never enough to ease the deep pain and writhing anguish hidden behind so many deadpan faces.

A deep and constant rapport with a compassionate companion is what we seek: someone who will be there night and day, through hellish high waters, offering us assurance that all will be well, even when our most trusted friends and mentors, counselors, and therapists are out of reach.

This can be found only in a relationship with a Spiritual Presence. And that relationship emerges from our Primal Prayer.

Primal Prayer is the primordial practice of
combining silence, sound, and movement
to invoke and communicate with a Spiritual Presence,
precipitating hope and healing.
For some, this Presence is a saint.
For others, it is an angel, or an animal.
For some, it is the lingering manifest memory of someone once loved, but now long gone.
For others, it is the sense of something beholden in the here and now.
For some, it is an apprehension of God or a celestial semblance.
For others, it is a numinous, innumerable, and immeasurable vitality that sometimes seems absent, though it is always known to be.
Yet, for everyone, it is a marvelous attendance, which speaks to us and hears our response, eliciting awe and hastening deep healing through a magnificent encounter.

Reviving and recovering this original practice of prayer, which predates and transcends divisions between faiths and religions, can make a life-saving difference to those facing severe distress or major challenges.

Prayer has the power to heal people, and unite diverse communities in a common cause to celebrate the unifying power of the Creative Spirit, joining atheists, agnostics, and fundamentally committed members of all religious traditions in a common expression of grace, peace, compassion, mutual love, and awe at what we are capable of creating.

At this time, I propose that nothing could be more urgently necessary than to find such internal harmony, collaborative creativity, and common ground between unnecessarily polarized people.

Paul Newham - 14 February 2015

Thus, my work brings prayer forth from the shadows, and illuminates the vital sustenance it has to offer, without recourse to exclusively theological principles, or religious dogma.

Yet, it also promotes a practice that is truly multi-faith, holding in high regard the specific quiddity of beliefs that characterize distinct religions.
